# Invisible Flows · 无形之流

> A bilingual field guide to underground migration around modern China.

A documentary-style analytical site mapping six regional patterns of irregular migration and cross-border movement around the PRC's 22,000-km land frontier and its long coastline — Xinjiang, Yunnan/Myanmar, Fujian–Zhejiang–Guangdong, the labor-export Northeast, the Guangxi/Hainan border trade, and the Himalayan/Tibet frontier — alongside a historical timeline, an analytical taxonomy of underground infrastructure, and a governance/future panel.

**Editorial position:** analytical, victim-forward, drawn from public sources (IOM, UNODC, GI-TOC, USIP, Sheldon Zhang, Frank Pieke, Adrian Zenz, AP, Reuters, NYT, ProPublica, China Labour Bulletin). The site **does not** endorse, instruct, or romanticize any of the activity it describes. No routes. No methods. No operational detail.

## Sections

- **00 Editorial banner + 01 Hero** — Six global/regional baselines
- **02 Xinjiang** — The securitized frontier (Central Asia)
- **03 Yunnan** — The Mekong gray zone (Myanmar compounds)
- **04 Clan migration** — Fujian/Zhejiang/Guangdong chain & snakehead economy
- **05 Labor migration** — Northeast + Hebei/Shandong/Jiangsu dispatch
- **06 Border trade** — Guangxi & Hainan maritime gray flows
- **07 Religious** — Tibet/Himalayan exile & pilgrimage
- **08 Historical timeline** — Silk Road → Compound era → Biometric perimeter
- **09 Underground infrastructure** — Six-layer analytical taxonomy
- **10 Governance & future** — AI surveillance, biometric identity, financial chokepoints
- **11 Sources + Closing** — Public references and a reflective frame

- Next.js 14 (App Router)
- Framer Motion — scroll reveals + animated route arcs
- Tailwind — bespoke industrial-investigative design system
- Bilingual EN/中 toggle (localStorage + browser locale)
- Spectral display + Inter body + JetBrains Mono data + Noto Serif/Sans SC for Han
- Pure SVG route maps (per pattern) and timeline
